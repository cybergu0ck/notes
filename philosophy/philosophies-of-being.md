## Essentialism

Essentialism is the view that objects have a set of attributes that are necessary to their identity.

- The origins of essentialism can be traced back to ancient Greek philosophers Plato and Aristotle, with their theories of natural forms and immutable essences.
- Example: A human’s essence is rationality; a knife’s essence is its capacity to cut.

## Existentialism

Existentialism is the philosophical viewpoint that one is responsible for creating purpose or meaning in life.

- Individual purpose, meaning, importance, morality is not given to us by Gods, releigion, governments, teachers or other authorities.
- Existentialists believe that the nature of existence varies and is individualized to each person.
- It is focused on free will to determine the course of lives.
- Key thinkers: Kierkegaard, Sartre, Camus, Heidegger.

## Nihilism

Nihilism is a philosophical viewpoint that rejects all values and beliefs, arguing that life is without objective meaning, purpose, or intrinsic value

- Key figure: Friedrich Nietzsche (though he also tried to overcome nihilism).

Roots: Ancient atomists (Democritus, Epicurus), revived in modern science and philosophy of mind.

## Hedonism

Pleasure is the highest good and the main purpose of life; pain is the greatest evil.

- The idea goes back to Aristippus of Cyrene, who taught that immediate, physical pleasure was the goal of life.
